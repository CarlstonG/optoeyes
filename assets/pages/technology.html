WHAT IS

OCT Technology?

OCT (optical coherence tomography) is a new imaging technology that uses light to capture images of the eye. With this technology we are able to scan deeper than before allowing us to detect, monitor and manage eye diseases at an earlier stage than previously possible. These images can also be rendered to create a 3-D representation of the eye which allows or better visualization. The biggest advantage is the Examinations take roughly 15 minutes with results available on same day. OCT is a considered the gold standard in monitoring and detecting many eye diseases such as macular degeneration and glaucoma.

Topcon CV-5000s

The Topcon CV-5000s gives our optometrists a faster way to perform refractions (the process of choosing the strength of glasses and contact lenses). It can be operated by a remote control meaning it can be operated accurately from a distance of 1.5m or more. This also saves time from a patient's perspective giving ensuring a more comfortable eye exam experience.

Medmont Visual Field Analyser

The Medmont Visual Field Analyser is a computerised eye exam. It helps check for glaucoma as it's progression. It can also be used to check your driving field of vision for those who have special conditions on their driving licence.
